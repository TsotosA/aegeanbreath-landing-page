Getting Started

Welcome to Aegean Breath! When you first launch the app, you'll see a quick onboarding guide. We recommend going through it to understand the basics.

Permissions

To provide the best experience, the app may ask for a few permissions:

  • Bluetooth: Required if you want to connect a heart rate monitor.
  • Notifications: Used to show the current phase and time remaining in the notification bar while you're training, so you can lock your screen or use other apps.

Home Screen Overview

The home screen is your central dashboard. From here, you can access all major features:

  • 1
    Max Breath Hold Card: Shows your personal best time. Tap "New Attempt" to record a new max hold.
  • 2
    Guided Tables: This is where you create and manage your training tables (CO2 and O2).
  • 3
    Quick Access: Your most recently used tables will appear here for one-tap access.
  • 4
    History & Settings: Access your training logs and app preferences via the cards at the bottom.

Creating Tables

Tables are the core training method for freediving. You can create three types of tables:

CO2 Tolerance

Builds tolerance to high CO2 levels. Rest times decrease while hold times stay constant.

O2 Tolerance

Adapts your body to low oxygen. Hold times increase while rest times stay constant.

Auto-Generation

The easiest way to start is using Auto-Generate. When creating a table, toggle the "Auto Generate" switch. The app will use your "Max Breath Hold" time to calculate a personalized table for you. You can adjust the difficulty slider to find the right level.

Note: You must record a Max Breath Hold first for this feature to work.

Training Sessions

When you start a table, "Alfie" (our audio guide) will lead you through the session.

Phases

  • Breathe (Prep): Relax and breathe normally. Prepare for the hold.
  • Hold: Stop breathing. Relax every muscle.

Contraction Tracking

During the Hold phase, a "Contraction" button will appear. Tap this button each time you feel a diaphragm contraction. This helps you analyze your struggle phase later.

Audio & Haptics

Max Breath Hold

This is a simple stopwatch to test your maximum breath-hold duration. It's crucial for the auto-generation algorithm.

To record: Tap "New Attempt", then tap anywhere on the screen to start the timer. Tap again to stop. Save your result if you beat your previous best!

Heart Rate Monitor

Aegean Breath supports standard Bluetooth Low Energy (BLE) heart rate monitors (like chest straps or armbands). Connecting one gives you valuable insight into your Mammalian Dive Reflex.

How to Connect:

  1. Go to Settings.
  2. Scroll to "Heart Rate Monitor" and tap Scan.
  3. Select your device from the list.
  4. Once connected, your heart rate will appear on the Home screen and during training sessions.

History & Progress

Every completed session is saved to your history. You can view your logs in a list or on a calendar.

Tap on any session to see detailed stats, including the total duration, notes you added, and your heart rate graph (if a monitor was connected).

Safety Warning

NEVER practice breath holding in water alone.

Even in a pool, shallow water blackout can be fatal. Aegean Breath is designed for dry training only. Always train lying down on a soft surface like a bed or sofa. If you faint (blackout), you should fall safely onto a soft surface where you can breathe naturally.
